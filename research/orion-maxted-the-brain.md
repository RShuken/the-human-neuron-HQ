# Research: Orion Maxted — [The Brain] Theatre of Mind

## The Direct Predecessor to The Human Neuron

**Artist:** Orion Maxted — Amsterdam-based theatre/performance maker. Studied Computing before turning to Music and Performance Art. Master of Theater from DAS (formerly DasArts). First artistic fellow at University of Amsterdam Institute for Advanced Studies (2019/2020).

**Collaborator:** Professor Francis Heylighen (complexity/cybernetics, VUB Brussels)

## The Installation

- ~100 audience members connect via smartphones (web app, instructions on a flyer)
- Audience transformed into "a single communal brain" — collective intelligence / swarm intelligence
- What audience collectively types on phones appears projected on stage
- The collective brain "learns to think, ask and answer questions, and be creative together"
- Performance begins with "Hello world you are beautiful"
- Maxted describes it as "algorithmic theatre" — "computers and language systems made of people"
- Built on neuroscience, cybernetics, and network theory
- Audience members positioned as nodes in a neural network

## How The Human Neuron Differs
| Maxted's [The Brain] | The Human Neuron |
|---|---|
| Letters one at a time | Words (token-level, like actual LLMs) |
| Collective text generation | Explicit neural network mechanics (weights, pathways, activation) |
| "You are a brain" framing | "You are a neuron" — individual role, collective emergence |
| Artistic/theatrical | Educational + artistic — teaches real AI concepts |
| Free-form text | Structured rounds showing forward pass, backprop, weight adjustment |
| No visible network | Real-time network visualization showing each person's neuron |
